What is Konviv?

Konviv is a financial empowerment company working to make sure people who have traditionally been marginalized from traditional products and services get to participate too. Our vision is one of a world where all are included in financial products and services, and these products are empowering and life-sustaining.

We are starting by building a money management app that connects to a chatbot. Initially we aim to work with those who are living paycheck-to-paycheck, and those that are struggling financially. Konviv allows people to manage their money in a way that is more intuitive, and encourages our users to contact our friendly bot, Viva, any time they have a question about their finances.

Deep Datta - Chief Operating Officer

Like many Americans, Deep's parents are immigrants. Arun (Dad) and Purabi (Mom) Datta were born into third world poverty in India. Both of Deep's parents educated themselves out of poverty by obtaining PhD's and moving to the United States to give their children the American dream. Deep has spent his entire life trying to understand how to use new media tools and internet technology to educate, raise awareness of, and connect people living in different parts of the world. He has worked for an Egyption Ambassador, enjoyed a corporate career at Thomson Reuters, worked with an artificial intelligence ad startup where he was implementing multimillion-dollar advertising campaigns for the world's biggest companies, and is currently working at a humanitarian technology lab helping to build software for vulnerable communities.

“About one in five Latinos lack access to the most basic financial services, and rely instead on high-cost, fringe providers to meet their financial needs.”

Meet Rosa. Like many Latinos struggling with finances, when Rosa falls short on cash she goes to a payday loan. Payday loans, a common way of getting money when one is in a crunch, typically have an annual interest rate between 391% to 521%. With Konviv, Rosa would always know where she stands and what things she can afford and not afford. She would never have to guess if her financial decisions might again send her to get a payday loan - with Konviv, she could make better decisions.
